📋 Findings & Recommendations
🏆 Category Performance
Clothing leads at $104,264, more than Footwear and Outerwear combined. Accessories follows at $74,200. Together, Clothing & Accessories account for 77% of all revenue.
💡 Prioritize inventory and marketing spend on Clothing and Accessories. Re-evaluate Outerwear pricing or seasonal timing.
📅 Seasonal Trends
Revenue is stable across seasons — Fall leads at $60,018 and Summer trails at $55,777. The gap is only ~$4,200, indicating low seasonal dependency.
💡 A modest Summer promotion could close the gap. No dramatic seasonal overhaul needed.
⭐ Ratings by Category
Customer satisfaction is consistently ~3.75 across all categories. Footwear scores highest at 3.79, Clothing lowest at 3.72 — a narrow 0.07 gap.
💡 Outerwear's underperformance is demand/pricing driven, not satisfaction driven. Ratings are unlikely to be a lever here.
🔔 Subscription Status
Non-subscribers generate 73% of revenue ($170,436) vs 27% from subscribers ($62,645). Average spend per transaction is nearly identical ($59.87 vs $59.49).
💡 Subscribers aren't spending more per purchase. The value lies in frequency. Test subscriber-only perks to grow this loyal segment.
🏷️ Promo Code Usage
43% of purchases (1,677 of 3,900) used a promo code — closely tracking discount usage. Promos drive meaningful purchase volume.
💡 Test whether promo usage actually changes order size, not just frequency. This clarifies whether discounting cuts margins or expands revenue.
🗺️ Geographic Insights
Montana, Illinois, and California are the top-revenue states. Revenue is distributed across all 50 states with no single dominant market.
💡 Explore targeted regional campaigns in high-performing states. Kansas trails significantly — investigate if it's demand or distribution.
